# Supervision Documentation Best Practices for LPCs and Psychologists

Supervision isn't just about getting feedback — it's about documenting that feedback happened. For licensure, insurance, and legal protection, your supervision records need to be complete and defensible.

## What Boards Actually Want to See

State licensing boards vary, but most require documentation showing:

- **Who**: Supervisor name, credentials, license number
- **What**: Topics discussed, recordings reviewed
- **When**: Date and duration of supervision
- **How**: Method of review (live, recorded, etc.)

If you use video recordings, you need proof the supervisor actually watched them — not just that they had access.

## The Documentation Gap

Most therapists track:
- ✅ Date of supervision meeting
- ✅ Supervisor credentials
- ✅ General topics covered

But miss:
- ❌ Which specific recordings were reviewed
- ❌ Proof the supervisor accessed them
- ❌ How much of each recording was watched
- ❌ When the review occurred

This gap matters if your license is questioned or you're in a legal dispute.

## Best Practice: The View Receipt

A proper view receipt includes:

- **Recording identifier** (filename or ID)
- **Access timestamp** (when they started watching)
- **Duration** (how long they viewed)
- **Completion status** (did they watch the full session?)
- **Access method** (IP address hash for audit trail)

With this, your documentation is complete. Without it, you're relying on your supervisor's memory and good faith.

## Sample Supervision Log Entry

```
Date: January 15, 2026
Supervisor: Dr. Jane Smith, PhD, LPC-S #12345
Method: Video review + discussion

Recordings Reviewed:
- Session_2026-01-10_ClientA.mov
  - Viewed: Jan 15, 2026 at 2:34 PM
  - Duration: 47 minutes (full session)
  - Proof: [View Receipt ID: abc123]

- Session_2026-01-12_ClientB.mov
  - Viewed: Jan 15, 2026 at 3:15 PM
  - Duration: 23 minutes (partial)
  - Proof: [View Receipt ID: def456]

Discussion Topics:
- Transference patterns in Client A
- Risk assessment for Client B
- Countertransference awareness

Supervisor Signature: _________________
```

## Tools That Help

**Manual tracking**: Spreadsheet + email confirmation
- Pros: Free
- Cons: Time-consuming, easy to forget, no proof

**Practice management software**: Built-in supervision notes
- Pros: Integrated with client records
- Cons: No view receipts, expensive

**Specialized video platforms**: Designed for supervision
- Pros: Automatic view receipts, encrypted sharing
- Cons: Another tool to learn (minimal learning curve)

## Red Flags to Avoid

❌ **"I emailed the video to my supervisor"**
- Email is not HIPAA-compliant for PHI

❌ **"My supervisor confirmed they watched it"**
- Verbal confirmation isn't documentation

❌ **"The file is in our shared Dropbox"**
- Personal Dropbox isn't a BAA-covered entity

❌ **"I trust my supervisor"**
- Documentation protects both of you

## Implementation Checklist

Switch to defensible documentation:

- [ ] Choose a HIPAA-aligned video sharing tool
- [ ] Require view receipts for every recording shared
- [ ] Log receipts with date, time, duration, completion
- [ ] Store logs with your other supervision records
- [ ] Review logs quarterly to ensure completeness

## The Bottom Line

Good supervision documentation isn't about mistrust — it's about professionalism. When you can prove exactly what was reviewed and when, you protect your license, your supervisor, and your clients.

The 10 minutes you spend setting up proper view receipts will save you hours of anxiety if anyone ever questions your supervision.
