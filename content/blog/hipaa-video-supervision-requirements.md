# HIPAA Video Supervision Requirements: A Practical Guide for Therapists

As a therapist, you're likely recording video sessions for supervision, consultation, or training purposes. But are you handling these recordings in a HIPAA-compliant way? This guide breaks down what you actually need to know.

## What HIPAA Requires for Video Recordings

HIPAA's Security Rule requires that electronic protected health information (ePHI) — which includes video recordings of therapy sessions — be:

- **Encrypted** both in transit and at rest
- **Access-controlled** with audit logs
- **Backed up** with recovery procedures
- **Properly disposed of** when no longer needed

## The Supervision Video Problem

Most therapists face a specific challenge: sharing session recordings with supervisors. Common workarounds include:

- Email (not HIPAA-compliant)
- Google Drive (business associate agreement required)
- Encrypted USB drives (secure but inconvenient)
- Practice management platforms (expensive, limited features)

Each of these has compliance gaps or workflow friction.

## What "HIPAA-Aligned" Actually Means

You don't need perfect compliance on day one. HIPAA-aligned means:

1. **Encryption**: AES-256 or equivalent
2. **Access controls**: Password protection, expiration dates
3. **Audit trails**: Who accessed what, when
4. **Data minimization**: Only store what you need
5. **Business associate considerations**: Who can see the data

## The View Receipt Solution

For supervision specifically, the critical compliance gap is **documentation**. You need proof that:

- The supervisor accessed the recording
- They reviewed it (not just downloaded it)
- The access was within your agreed timeframe

Most platforms give you "link opened" at best. What you actually need is a **view receipt** — timestamped proof of access with duration.

## Practical Checklist

Before sharing your next supervision video, verify:

- [ ] Recording is encrypted before upload
- [ ] Only you and your supervisor can decrypt it
- [ ] Link expires automatically after review
- [ ] You get a receipt when they watch it
- [ ] You can revoke access instantly if needed
